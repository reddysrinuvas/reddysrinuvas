Incecticides

Insecticides are chemical substances specifically designed to control or eliminate insects that pose a threat to crops, livestock, or human health. These pesticides target various stages of an insect's life cycle, disrupting their physiology or nervous system to prevent damage to agricultural produce or the spread of insect-borne diseases. Insecticides play a crucial role in modern agriculture by safeguarding crops from pests, thereby ensuring higher yields and food security. However, their use raises concerns about environmental impact, including potential harm to non-target organisms and the development of insecticide-resistant pests. The content of insecticides varies widely, encompassing synthetic chemicals, biological agents, and botanical extracts. Common categories include organophosphates, pyrethroids, neonicotinoids, and microbial insecticides. Each type has distinct mechanisms of action, persistence, and environmental effects.
