Groundcovers

Ground covers refer to low-growing plants that spread across the soil, forming a dense cover and effectively minimizing the exposure of bare ground. These plants play a crucial role in landscaping and gardening by providing various benefits. Ground covers help control soil erosion, suppress weed growth, and enhance the aesthetic appeal of outdoor spaces. They are commonly used in gardens, lawns, and other landscaped areas to create a uniform and visually pleasing ground surface. In addition to their practical functions, ground covers contribute to ecosystem health by promoting biodiversity and serving as habitats for small organisms. They come in a wide range of species, offering options for different climates and soil conditions. Whether used for erosion control on slopes, as a substitute for traditional lawns, or as decorative elements in garden beds, ground covers are versatile and serve both functional and ornamental purposes in landscaping.
