Shrubs

Shrubs typically refer to small to medium-sized woody plants with multiple stems, often branching out near the base. These plants are characterized by their shorter height compared to trees and are commonly found in gardens, landscapes, and natural environments. Shrubs play a crucial role in landscaping, providing aesthetic appeal, defining borders, and contributing to overall ecosystem health. They come in various shapes, sizes, and foliage, offering a diverse range of options for ornamental and functional purposes. In terms of content, the term "shrubs" can also refer to a type of beverage known as "shrubs" or "drinking vinegars." These are concoctions made by combining fruit, sugar, and vinegar, creating a sweet and tangy syrup. Mixed with water or soda, shrubs make refreshing drinks with a unique flavor profile.
